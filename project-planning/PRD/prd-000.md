## 1. Executive Summary

**Component Usage Atlas** is a Sitecore Marketplace app that gives content editors a **live, tenant-wide view of where renderings and their datasources are actually used**. It ships on two surfaces simultaneously:

- **Dashboard Widget** — *component-centric*: search any rendering, see every page that uses it.
- **Page Context Panel** — *page-centric*: for the current page, list the renderings on it and how many other pages share each one (with click-through to the cross-tenant list).

The headline use case is **pre-change impact analysis**. When an editor is about to publish, modify, or delete content, they open the panel/widget to answer *"if I do this, what else breaks?"* — without leaving Pages.

The atlas runs **fully live, in-memory, in the iframe**: no backend, no persisted index, no scheduled jobs. A scan walks `xmc.agent.sitesGetSitesList → sitesGetAllPagesBySite → pagesGetComponentsOnPage` for the active tenant on demand. Wait times at the thousands-of-pages scale are absorbed by an **engaging, branded loading experience** (cancelable, with phase progress and a stretch-goal mini-game). The result is cached for the lifetime of the browser tab and refreshed manually or on remount.

This is the **third Marketplace dogfood app** in the `products/` portfolio (after `pageshot` and `quickcopy`) and the first to exercise **multi-step graph fetching across the tenant**. It is therefore the load-bearing test of the agent-endpoint pattern documented in `.agent/skills/sitecore/marketplace-sdk/xmc.md` § 13.

## 2. Problem Statement

Sitecore editors at the XM Cloud authoring layer make daily changes — publishing pages, modifying renderings, deleting content items — without a fast, in-context way to see **where else those things are used across the tenant**.

The pain shows up in three concrete moments:

1. **Pre-publish:** *"I just edited the HeroV2 datasource. What else points at this datasource?"* — Answer today: hand-traversal of `/sitecore/content` references, GraphQL queries in another tool, or "publish and hope."
2. **Pre-delete:** *"I want to remove the LegacyPromo rendering from our component palette. Is anyone still using it?"* — Answer today: case-by-case inspection or full-site crawls outside Pages.
3. **Pre-modify:** *"If I rename or restructure HeroV2, where will the changes propagate?"* — Answer today: tribal knowledge plus broken-page reports after deployment.

Native Sitecore Pages does not surface cross-page rendering usage with datasource awareness. Existing third-party indexers either require a paid service, depend on persistent infrastructure, or operate outside the editor's flow. Editors lose minutes to hours per change cycle, and high-blast-radius deletions get postponed indefinitely because nobody can prove they're safe.

**Root cause of the pain:** the *layout* (rendering placement, datasource bindings) of every page lives in Sitecore but is not aggregated and queryable from the editor's surface. The Marketplace SDK Agent endpoints expose the building blocks (`pagesGetComponentsOnPage`, `sitesGetAllPagesBySite`) — there just isn't an app that stitches them together into an editor-facing answer.

### Pain quantification — assumptions, not data

**Honest framing:** This PRD does not have hard interview data behind the magnitude claims. The following are **labeled assumptions**, to be validated post-launch via S1/S6 pulses and qualitative editor interviews. They are not load-bearing for v1 — the product ships if the qualitative jobs are real, even if the quantitative claim ("minutes to hours") turns out to be exaggerated.

- **A-PAIN-1:** Editors lose 5–60 minutes per pre-deletion verification cycle today. Source: estimation from internal Sitecore expertise; **not** from interview data. Validation: post-launch pulse comparing "time saved vs. previous workflow."
- **A-PAIN-2:** High-blast-radius deletions are postponed indefinitely because verification is hard. Source: hypothesis from product instinct. Validation: track whether the cleanup-mode opportunity in § 15 attracts inbound interest.
- **A-PAIN-3:** Datasource-change impact is the bigger blind spot than rendering-change impact (because rendering definitions are typically managed by developers via code, while datasources are edited by editors directly). This drives the equal billing of rendering and datasource impact in v1. Validation: relative click-through rates between the rendering drawer and the datasource drawer in S1 telemetry.
- **A-PAIN-4 — Trigger frequency ranking (assumption):** publish > modify > delete, in descending order of "how often does the editor want to check?" The Page Context Panel is therefore the more-frequently-opened surface; the Dashboard Widget serves the rarer but higher-stakes deletion / cleanup case. Polish weighting follows: panel first, widget close behind.
- **A-PAIN-5 — Pages-not-items abstraction line:** editors should not need to read item IDs in normal flows. Item IDs may appear as tooltips or copyable badges (debug-friendly) but never as the primary identifier in any list, drawer, or counter. Confirmed in § 4 and enforced in § 11.

### Competitive landscape — explicit gap

The team is not aware, as of 2026-04-27, of a free Marketplace app that surfaces tenant-wide component+datasource usage with the panel/widget split this PRD describes. **A-COMP-1 (assumption):** No equivalent exists in the Sitecore Marketplace today. **Validation:** check the Marketplace listing during `/architect`; if a competitor exists, revisit the differentiation in § 1 before further investment. Differentiation if a paid/persistent-infra competitor surfaces:
- Live, no-infra, no-license — install-and-go.
- In-context (panel) rather than dashboard-only.
- Datasource-aware in the same view.

## 4. Target Users

### Primary: **Content Editors**

The editor on the Sitecore Pages canvas, mid-task, who is about to publish/delete/modify and wants to know what else is affected. They are not technical implementers. They:

- Live inside Pages most of the working day
- Recognize rendering names by display name, not template ID
- Care about *pages*, not *items*
- Want a fast yes/no/list — not a graph database
- Will accept a 5–15s wait if the wait is well-paced, branded, and cancelable

### Secondary (consider, do not optimize for in v1)

- **Information architects** — would use the widget for component-rationalization audits. The component-centric search satisfies their needs incidentally; no separate UX track in v1.
- **Developers preparing migrations** — same overlap; if they want richer queries, they can build their own tool against the same agent endpoints.

### Explicitly **not** target users in v1

- Cross-tenant administrators (the "Standalone umbrella" pattern is out of scope)
- Localization specialists (cross-language usage is a phase-2 concern)
- Auditors needing historical/trend data (phase 2)

### Anti-persona — explicitly excluded

**The text-only editor.** An editor whose work is limited to copy edits on existing pages (no rendering placement, no datasource changes, no deletions) gains nothing from this app. The Dashboard Widget is **opt-in** at install time per Marketplace conventions, so admins should not install it for tenants where this is the dominant editor profile. The Page Context Panel is similarly low-cost to ignore (collapsed state by default).

### Persona claims — labeled as assumptions

The following persona claims are not interview-validated:

- **A-PERS-1** — *"editors live inside Pages most of the working day"*: assumption based on observed XM Cloud customer patterns; not validated for every tenant. Some customers still operate in legacy Content Editor; for those, panel-first design has lower reach.
- **A-PERS-2** — *"editors recognize rendering names by display name, not template ID"*: assumption based on standard editor UX. Display-name collisions are handled in IS-16.
- **A-PERS-3** — *"editors will accept 5–15s wait if well-paced"*: **assumption** — explicitly opened as OQ-1. Validation: S6 ("felt fast") and qualitative interviews post-launch.
- **A-PERS-4** — *"editors will accept the pull-only model"* (vs. expecting a blocking modal at delete time): **assumption flagged also as R10**. Validation: post-launch — if S5 (return rate) underperforms and qualitative feedback says "I keep forgetting to check before deleting," redesign cues in Phase 2.

### Permissions — explicit handling

Tenants with role-based scoping may grant agent-endpoint visibility to pages the active editor cannot navigate to. Atlas behavior:

- Pages returned by `xmc.agent.sitesGetAllPagesBySite` are scanned and indexed regardless of whether the active editor has navigation rights to them.
- **At click-through time**, the host frame's navigation contract enforces permissions; if the editor cannot open a page, Pages itself shows the appropriate error.
- A 403 on `xmc.agent.pagesGetComponentsOnPage` for a specific page → that page lands in `skipped[]` with reason `"forbidden"` and is excluded from counts (IS-17). It is **not** silently 0-counted.

This means the displayed counts are **upper bounds** for the editor's visible universe — never lower bounds. Editors with restricted scope will see equal-or-greater counts than they could reach by hand-traversal, which is the safer direction for impact analysis.

## 6. User Stories

### US-1 — Component-centric impact lookup (widget)

> **As** a content editor,
> **I want** to type a rendering's name into a tenant-wide widget and see every page that uses it,
> **so that** I can decide whether it's safe to deprecate, rename, or restructure that rendering.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- AC-1.1 Opening the widget for the first time in a tab triggers a scan with visible phase progress (Sites → Pages → Components).
- AC-1.2 The scan is cancelable; canceling shows partial results gathered so far with a clear "incomplete" badge.
- AC-1.3 After the scan, the widget shows a search-first table with columns: rendering display name, total usages, distinct pages, last-seen update timestamp (best available from agent endpoint, else "—"). When two renderings share a display name, second-and-subsequent rows render with a disambiguation suffix `Hero · <last-7-of-rendering-id>`; tooltip exposes the full rendering ID (per IS-16).
- AC-1.4 Typing in the search box filters the table client-side (no re-fetch).
- AC-1.5 Clicking a row opens a side drawer listing every page (path + display name) that uses the rendering, with a click-through that opens that page in Pages.
- AC-1.6 The drawer separates *direct rendering usage* from *usage via datasource* (two grouped sections).
- AC-1.7 A "Refresh atlas" action re-runs the scan; results before refresh remain visible until the new scan completes.

### US-2 — Page-centric impact lookup (panel)

> **As** a content editor on a specific page,
> **I want** the side panel to show the renderings on this page and tell me how many other pages use each one,
> **so that** I can spot before-publish risk without leaving Pages.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- AC-2.1 The panel mounts with the current page's renderings listed (rendering display name, datasource if any, parameters surfaced as a tooltip).
- AC-2.2 Each row shows a "+N other pages" counter once the tenant scan resolves; before scan completion, the counter shows a loading spinner inline.
- AC-2.3 Clicking the counter opens the same drawer pattern as US-1 (cross-tenant page list).
- AC-2.4 ~~Below the rendering list, a **"Datasource impact"** group lists each unique datasource item referenced from this page and its cross-tenant page count, with the same drawer click-through.~~ **(superseded by S22/S23, 2026-04-29 → 2026-04-30)** — Each rendering row in the panel is now expandable; expanding exposes the bound datasource inline with its own cross-tenant page-count counter and a click-through to the per-datasource drawer. The standalone "Datasource impact" group was removed because (a) the cross-tenant datasource counter only makes sense in the context of the rendering that binds it, and (b) the stacked layout broke the rendering↔datasource visual link past 20+ items. See `docs/architecture.md` § Page Context Panel.
- AC-2.5 If the page references a datasource missing from the tenant content tree, the row shows a warning badge ("Missing datasource — referenced by N pages").
- AC-2.6 The panel reuses the session-scoped scan from US-1 — opening the panel after the widget has already scanned does not re-scan.

### US-3 — Tenant-scope filter (collection narrowing)

> **As** a content editor in a multi-collection tenant,
> **I want** to scope the atlas to one collection (or all collections),
> **so that** I can ignore noise from sites I don't own.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- AC-3.1 A "Scope" control on both surfaces exposes: "All collections" (default) and one option per collection returned by `xmc.sites.listCollections`.
- AC-3.2 Changing scope mid-session triggers a fresh scan limited to sites in that collection. Previous results are kept until the new scan completes.
- AC-3.3 The selected scope persists for the tab session (not across tabs).
- AC-3.4 **Tenants with one collection:** the scope control renders disabled with helper text *"Only one collection in this tenant"*; default scope is that collection.
- AC-3.5 **Tenants with no collections (legacy / unconfigured):** the scope control is hidden; default and only scope is "All sites in this tenant."
- AC-3.6 **Tenants with collections that contain zero sites:** those collections are filtered out of the dropdown.

### US-4 — Engaging loading experience

> **As** a content editor staring at a multi-second scan,
> **I want** the wait to feel intentional, not broken,
> **so that** I trust the app and don't kill the panel.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- AC-4.1 The loading view always shows: current phase label, pages-completed / pages-total, elapsed time, cancel button.
- AC-4.2 No phase shows "0%" for more than 1.5s without a sub-task signal (e.g., "Fetching page 47 of 312").
- AC-4.3 A "Distract me" optional toggle reveals a branded mini-distraction (animation, mini-game — exact form is an open question for design). Default is off; preference persists per tab.
- AC-4.4 If a scan stalls (>5s with no progress), the UI shows a "Slow connection" hint with a "Try again" affordance.
- AC-4.5 Errors in individual page fetches don't fail the scan; they are counted and surfaced as a "Skipped N pages — view details" link in the final results.

### US-5 — Result freshness signaling

> **As** a content editor returning to the atlas later in the session,
> **I want** to know if the data is fresh,
> **so that** I don't act on stale results after I or someone else published.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- AC-5.1 The widget and panel both display "Last scanned: HH:MM" at the top.
- AC-5.2 If the elapsed time exceeds 15 minutes, the timestamp is shown with a "Stale?" hint and a one-click refresh.
- AC-5.3 The atlas does not auto-refresh in v1 (confirmed by editor-controlled freshness model).

## 7. Functional Requirements

### FR-1 — Tenant scan engine (shared)

- FR-1.1 Resolve `sitecoreContextId` from `application.context` using the guard-clause pattern from xmc.md § 12a; do not cast undefined.
- FR-1.2 Enumerate sites via `xmc.agent.sitesGetSitesList`.
- FR-1.3 For each site, enumerate pages via `xmc.agent.sitesGetAllPagesBySite` using the **site's default language** (per IS-13). If the site does not declare a default, fall back to the tenant default. Editor's UI language is not used.
- FR-1.4 For each page, fetch components via `xmc.agent.pagesGetComponentsOnPage`.
- FR-1.5 Aggregate results into two indices: `renderingIndex: Map<RenderingId, PageRef[]>` and `datasourceIndex: Map<DatasourceItemId, PageRef[]>`.
- FR-1.6 Use `Promise.allSettled` with a configurable concurrency limit (initial: 8) to avoid hammering the agent endpoint.
- FR-1.7 Surface per-page failures as counted "skipped" results, not fatal errors.
- FR-1.8 Apply collection-scope filter at the site enumeration step (filter sites by collection ID before fetching pages).

### FR-2 — Dashboard Widget surface

- FR-2.1 Mount the widget into `xmc:dashboardblocks` extension point.
- FR-2.2 On first mount in a tab, trigger a tenant scan via the shared engine.
- FR-2.3 Render search-first table with columns: rendering name, total usages, distinct pages, last-seen update.
- FR-2.4 Provide a side drawer detailing per-page usage when a row is clicked.
- FR-2.5 Provide a "Refresh atlas" button that re-runs the scan keeping prior results visible until completion.

### FR-3 — Page Context Panel surface

- FR-3.1 Mount the panel into `xmc:pages:context-panel` extension point.
- FR-3.2 Read the active page ID from the panel's context (per the SDK contract for that extension point).
- FR-3.3 Render the current page's rendering list using `xmc.agent.pagesGetComponentsOnPage` for the active page (independent of the full scan).
- FR-3.4 Once the full tenant scan resolves, populate "+N other pages" counters per rendering and per datasource.
- FR-3.5 ~~Render the "Datasource impact" group below the rendering list.~~ **(reshaped by S22/S23)** — Render each rendering row as expandable; expanding exposes the bound datasource inline with its own cross-tenant page-count counter and a click-through that opens the per-datasource drawer (see AC-2.4 superseding note).
- FR-3.6 Reuse the session-scoped scan; do not re-scan if one is already in memory.

### FR-4 — Session-scoped cache

- FR-4.1 Hold the scan result in a module-level singleton scoped to the SDK client lifetime.
- FR-4.2 Invalidate the cache on: explicit "Refresh atlas" action; scope change (collection filter switch); root unmount.
- FR-4.3 Do not invalidate on visibility-change or page navigation within Pages.

### FR-5 — Loading UX

- FR-5.1 Phase-aware progress component reused on both surfaces.
- FR-5.2 Cancel action aborts in-flight requests via an `AbortController` and freezes whatever is gathered so far.
- FR-5.3 "Distract me" optional UI element. Exact distraction TBD (open question § 14).

### FR-6 — Cross-page navigation

- FR-6.1 Page rows in drawers expose a click-through that uses the host frame's navigation contract to open that page in Pages (per `host-frame-testing.md` if needed; otherwise via the page edit URL pattern documented in the SDK).

### FR-7 — Error surfacing

- FR-7.1 Top-level scan errors (auth, missing context) render a clear error state with retry.
- FR-7.2 Per-page failures appear as a counted "Skipped N pages" link with a list of skipped pages and reasons in a sub-drawer. Reasons distinguish at least: `forbidden` (403), `timeout`, `not_found`, `network_error`, `other`.

### FR-8 — Datasource binding scope (per IS-12)

- FR-8.1 In v1, only datasources returned directly on the rendering's `Datasource` property by `xmc.agent.pagesGetComponentsOnPage` are recorded.
- FR-8.2 Inherited datasources (from page designs / partial designs), datasources resolved via personalization rules or A/B variant rules, and datasources expressed via rendering parameter tokens are **not** counted in v1.
- FR-8.3 The widget and panel each carry a small "ⓘ Direct bindings only" affordance with hover text explaining the v1 scope.

### FR-9 — Result-row disambiguation (per IS-16)

- FR-9.1 The widget table groups by rendering ID (not by display name).
- FR-9.2 If two or more renderings share a display name within the visible result set, all rows for those names render with a `· <last-7-of-id>` suffix; tooltips show the full ID.
- FR-9.3 The Page Context Panel rendering list applies the same disambiguation when the active page contains two distinct renderings with the same display name.

## 8. Non-Functional Requirements

### NFR-1 — Performance

- NFR-1.1 First scan on ~1k pages completes in under 30s on a typical broadband connection (target; verify in QA).
- NFR-1.2 Search/filter inside the widget is instant (≤50ms) — purely client-side after scan.
- NFR-1.3 Re-mount of widget or panel after scan completion shows results in <200ms.

### NFR-2 — Scalability

- NFR-2.1 The scan engine handles tenants with thousands of pages without UI freeze (results stream into the indices; UI remains responsive).
- NFR-2.2 Concurrency cap is configurable; default 8.

### NFR-3 — Reliability

- NFR-3.1 Partial-failure tolerance: a single page failure does not abort the scan.
- NFR-3.2 If `application.context` is unavailable, the app shows a clear "App is not bound to a tenant" message and does not attempt scans.
- NFR-3.3 No silent-fail paths: every error is either retried, counted, or surfaced.

### NFR-4 — Accessibility

- NFR-4.1 Keyboard navigation on the widget table (arrow keys to move rows, Enter to open drawer, Escape to close).
- NFR-4.2 Loading-phase progress is announced via `aria-live` for screen readers.
- NFR-4.3 Color is never the only signal (skipped pages, warnings, etc. all carry text).

### NFR-5 — Security

- NFR-5.1 No tokens in client code; rely on portal-brokered auth via Mode A.
- NFR-5.2 No external network calls outside `@sitecore-marketplace-sdk/*` (confirmed by build-time check).
- NFR-5.3 No PII in friction-log or telemetry — page IDs and rendering IDs only.

### NFR-6 — Observability

- NFR-6.1 Scan timing, page count, skipped count are written to a friction log per the run-manifest standard.
- NFR-6.2 Errors include endpoint, params, and a sanitized error message.

### NFR-7 — Deployment

- NFR-7.1 Single Marketplace app registration covers both extension points.
- NFR-7.2 No external infrastructure (DB, function app, cron) required.
- NFR-7.3 Static-hostable build (Vite + React or marketplace-app starter); deploy target pinned in architecture phase.

### NFR-8 — Maintainability

- NFR-8.1 SDK calls live behind a single client wrapper module.
- NFR-8.2 Index-building logic is pure (input: raw agent responses; output: indices) and unit-testable without the SDK.

## 9. Technical Considerations

### 9.1 — SDK and APIs

- **Mode:** Mode A (iframe + `ClientSDK.init({ modules: [XMC] })`) per `.agent/skills/sitecore/marketplace-sdk/xmc.md` §§ 2a, 2c.
- **Extension points:** `xmc:dashboardblocks` (widget), `xmc:pages:context-panel` (panel) — both tenant-scoped (single `resourceAccess[]` entry).
- **Endpoints used:**
  - `application.context` — tenant binding, `sitecoreContextId`
  - `xmc.agent.sitesGetSitesList`
  - `xmc.sites.listCollections` (for the collection-scope filter)
  - `xmc.agent.sitesGetAllPagesBySite`
  - `xmc.agent.pagesGetComponentsOnPage` (for both the per-page rendering list AND the tenant scan)
  - Optional: `xmc.agent.pagesGetPage` for richer page metadata if needed by the drawer
- **Data-fetching pattern:** the recommended pattern in xmc.md § 13 is the spine of the scan engine. Concurrency, guard-clause for `sitecoreContextId`, `Promise.allSettled` for partial-failure tolerance — all enforced.

### 9.2 — Architecture posture

- No backend. No persisted store. No scheduled job.
- All state lives in the iframe's JS heap.
- A single SDK client instance per surface; both surfaces share the same module-singleton cache.

### 9.3 — Stack

- **Not** a Content SDK / XM Cloud head app. The CLAUDE.md scaffold rule (`.agent/skills/sitecore/setup/scaffold.md`) is **not applicable here** — this is a Marketplace app, not a head/rendering host.
- Setup follows `.agent/skills/sitecore/marketplace-sdk/` (architect chooses Vite + React or the marketplace-app starter; record in ADR).
- TypeScript-first per `10-language.mdc`. No new heavy dependencies beyond the SDK packages, a UI primitive library (TBD), and a router only if the widget/panel routing requires one.

### 9.4 — Identity model

- **Rendering identity** = rendering definition item ID (the template-level component definition). Two instances of HeroV2 on different pages share the same rendering ID.
- **Datasource identity** = item ID of the bound content item.
- **Page reference (`PageRef`)** = `{ pageId, pageName, sitePath, siteId, language }`.
- Display names come from agent endpoints when available; fallback to ID with a "(no name)" badge.

### 9.5 — Concurrency and rate limits

- Initial concurrency cap: 8 simultaneous `pagesGetComponentsOnPage` calls.
- If the agent endpoint surfaces 429-style errors at scale, fall back to exponential backoff with jitter, not silent retry-loops.
- Cancel via `AbortController` shared across all in-flight requests.

### 9.6 — Out-of-band: Sitecore Pages cannot be intercepted

- The Marketplace SDK does not allow apps to intercept publish/delete actions in Pages.
- The product is therefore **pull-only**. This is a hard constraint, not a v1 limitation.
- The "loading game" idea is a deliberate response to this: if we can't shorten the wait, we can shape it.

## 10. Data Model Overview

### 10.1 — In-memory atlas

```ts
type RenderingId = string;       // rendering definition item ID
type DatasourceId = string;      // content item ID
type PageId = string;

type PageRef = {
  pageId: PageId;
  pageName: string;
  sitePath: string;
  siteId: string;
  siteName: string;
  language: string;
  collectionId?: string;
};

type RenderingUsage = {
  pages: PageRef[];
  totalUsages: number;             // pages.length unless one page uses it twice
  datasources: Set<DatasourceId>;  // distinct datasources bound to this rendering
};

type DatasourceUsage = {
  pages: PageRef[];                // pages where any rendering binds this datasource
  renderings: Set<RenderingId>;    // which renderings reference it
};

type Atlas = {
  scope: 'all-collections' | { collectionId: string };
  scannedAt: string;               // ISO timestamp
  renderingIndex: Map<RenderingId, RenderingUsage>;
  datasourceIndex: Map<DatasourceId, DatasourceUsage>;
  skipped: Array<{ pageId: PageId; reason: string }>;
  totals: { sites: number; pages: number; renderings: number; datasources: number };
};
```

### 10.2 — Build pipeline

1. Resolve context.
2. Sites list → filtered by scope.
3. Pages list per site → flattened to `PageRef[]`.
4. Components per page → for each component, append to `renderingIndex[renderingId].pages` and (if datasource present) to `datasourceIndex[datasourceId].pages`.
5. Compute totals.
6. Freeze the atlas object; UI consumes via selectors.

### 10.3 — No persistence

No IndexedDB, no localStorage, no external store. The atlas dies with the tab. This is a deliberate v1 simplification — see ADR (to be created in step 12 of `/create-prd`) for the decision rationale.

## 11. UX Considerations

### 11.1 — Surface treatments

- **Dashboard Widget:** full-width within the dashboard block; search bar pinned top; results table fills the remaining height; drawer slides from the right.
- **Page Context Panel:** narrow column; current-page rendering list at top; **each rendering row expandable to reveal its bound datasource inline** with its own cross-tenant counter (S22/S23 reshape — replaces the original "Datasource impact section below" pattern); counters update in place when scan resolves; clicking the rendering's "See all pages →" affordance opens the per-rendering drawer; clicking the datasource line opens the per-datasource drawer.

### 11.2 — Visual identity

- Branded loading experience consistent with the existing dogfood apps (`pageshot`, `quickcopy`).
- The "Distract me" toggle is the headline visual differentiator. Concept: a subtle scanning animation by default; a small mini-game when toggled.
- Dark + light mode parity required (Pages supports both).

### 11.3 — Empty / edge states

- **No tenant context:** "Atlas needs a tenant connection — please reload the dashboard."
- **Empty tenant (no sites or pages):** "This tenant has no published pages yet."
- **Search with zero matches:** "No renderings match `<term>`. Try a partial name."
- **Scan canceled:** "Partial results — N pages of M scanned. Re-scan?"
- **All renderings show 1 usage:** "This tenant has no shared renderings yet — every component is unique to a page."

### 11.4 — Accessibility checklist (non-exhaustive)

- Tab order is logical from search → table → drawer.
- All counters and badges have text labels.
- `aria-live` for scan progress.
- No drag-only interactions.

### 11.5 — Loading game (open)

Possible directions (decide in `/architect` UI design step):
- A subtle generative-art bloom that "grows" as pages complete (low effort, high polish)
- A Snake / Pong / Breakout micro-game
- A "spot the rendering" themed flip-card mini-game
- The user explicitly endorsed "a nice loading animation or game" in discovery — they're delighted, not just tolerant.

## 13. Risks and Mitigations

| ID | Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|----|------|------------|--------|-----------|
| R1 | Scan time at >2k-page tenants exceeds editor patience even with engaging loading | Medium | High | Concurrency tuning; "scan visible site only" quick mode (US-3); persistence (Phase 3) |
| R2 | Agent endpoint rate-limits at scale | Medium | Medium | Concurrency cap; backoff with jitter; surface throttling errors clearly |
| R3 | Rendering identity mismatch (instance vs definition); datasource cloning across language fallbacks | Medium | Medium | Document mapping rules in ADR; normalize on item ID; QA across multilingual tenants |
| R4 | SDK breaking changes between releases | Low | Medium | Pin SDK version; maintain a friction-log entry per SDK upgrade |
| R5 | "Stale" cache misleads editor after a peer publishes | Medium | Low | Stale-time hint at >15min (AC-5.2); manual refresh always available |
| R6 | Editor cancels long scans habitually and never sees full results | Medium | Medium | UX research in `/architect`; consider a "background scan" mode in Phase 2 |
| R7 | Mini-game distraction backfires (looks unprofessional, costs perf) | Low | Low | Off by default; tab-scoped preference; lightweight implementation |
| R8 | Page Context Panel extension point context shape varies between SDK versions | Low | Medium | Lock SDK version for v1; record verified version in CATALOG run row |
| R9 | Tenant has very deep page hierarchies that cause the agent endpoint to time out | Low | Medium | Per-page timeout with "skipped" classification; do not block scan |
| R10 | Editor expects deletion-blocking modal (per discovery: "all the time when publishing/deleting/modifying") and is disappointed by pull-only | Medium | High | Lead with "open the panel before you act" UX language; landing copy that frames the app as a check-before-action tool. **Validation hook:** monitor S5 return rate and qualitative pulse for "I keep forgetting to check before deleting" — if signal appears, design Phase-2 reminder cues (e.g., "Has anything changed?" prompt on panel open after a delete). |
| R11 | Pulse-based success metrics (S1, S6) hit too few responses to be statistically meaningful at small install base | Medium | Medium | Validity floors documented per metric (≥30 responses); pulse appears at most once per session; below-floor results reported as "preliminary" not "validated" |
| R12 | Direct-binding-only datasource scope (IS-12) under-counts impact in tenants that rely heavily on page designs / partial designs | Medium | Medium | Editor-visible "ⓘ Direct bindings only" affordance; OS-4 promotion to Phase 2 is gated on inbound feedback signal |
| R13 | Display-name collisions surface as suffix-disambiguated rows that confuse editors expecting one row per name | Low | Low | UX research in `/architect`; tooltip + ID badge; consider grouped collapsing in Phase 2 |
| R14 | Forbidden-page handling silently inflates counts vs. what the editor can act on | Low | Low | Counts are framed as "upper bound for visibility"; tooltip on counter explains scope; Phase 2 may add "show only pages I can navigate" toggle |

## 14. Open Questions

These are explicitly open and to be resolved in `/architect` or design phases. The PRD does not commit to answers here.

- **OQ-1:** Editor patience threshold for first-load. Is 30s on 1k pages acceptable? What about 60s on 5k? — answer informs concurrency targets and the Phase-3 persistence trigger.
- **OQ-2:** Loading visual treatment in v1 (the mini-game itself is OS-12) — generative art, evolving progress visualization, or branded animation only? Decide in UI design step of `/architect`.
- **OQ-3:** Mid-scan page-switch behavior in the panel — abort and re-scan for the new page only, or keep the global scan running and just swap the per-page view? Recommend the latter; verify in design.
- **OQ-4:** Should scope-change (collection filter) re-use already-fetched pages from a wider scan, or always re-scan from scratch? Reuse is cheaper but risks staleness.
- **OQ-5:** Dark/light mode source of truth — read from the host frame, or follow OS preference? Confirm with `host-frame-testing.md` once available.
- **OQ-6:** What's the minimum agent-endpoint set we can assume on every XM Cloud tenant in the install base? Confirm at architect-time against the SDK's stability tier.
- **OQ-7:** Friction-log destination — local file (per current dogfood pattern) or surfaced in-app for editor self-report?
- **OQ-8:** Do we expose a "what changed since last scan?" diff in v1 if the scan is cheap enough, or save it for Phase 2?
- **OQ-9:** Handling of pages with renderings that have no rendering definition reference (legacy / orphan placements) — show as "unknown rendering" or skip?
- **OQ-10:** Telemetry for the success metrics (S1–S6) — strictly client-side or do we pull anything via Marketplace install analytics if/when exposed by the platform?
- **OQ-11:** Pulse instrument design — single one-question prompt vs. two questions (S1 + S6 separately) vs. star rating + free text. Risk: too many questions tank response rate; too few miss signal.
- **OQ-12:** S5 (return rate) instrumentation — is there any Marketplace install analytics we can read at all, or does S5 stay aspirational until the platform exposes it?
- **OQ-13:** Anti-metric guard — who runs the per-release sign-off check that no anti-metric is on a dashboard? Likely the Team Lead during `/ship`; confirm during `/architect`.
